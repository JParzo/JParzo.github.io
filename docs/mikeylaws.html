The Michaelman Laws

In accordance with Official OGOB documentation

Section 1: Michael-related offences

  • Section 1.(a)(i): You must not take the Michael. A Michael is the highest offence punishable under OGOB Law.

  • Section 1.(a)(ii): You must not take the Mickey. A Mickey is the intermediate offence and can be escalated towards a Michael-taking situation if needs must.

  • Section 1.(a)(iii): You must not take the Mick. A Mick is the lowest offence and can be escalated towards a Mickey-taking situation if needs must.

    • Failure to comply with:

  • Section 1.(a)(i): The offender has to be signed onto the Michaelman register and receives a certificate insuring their offence is well known and documented.

  • Section 1.(a)(ii): The offender is given a verbal warning and could escalate to a Michael offence

  • Section 1.(a)(iii): The offender is given a verbal warning and could escalate to a Mickey offence


Section 4: Register-related issues

  • Section 4.(b): Anyone located on the Michaelman register cannot take the Michael again and will be on a strict watch.

Section 69: Upholding the Michaelman Laws

  • Section 69.(f): The Michaelman laws are subject to change on a case by case nature and are set by precedence where applicable. Only Sections 1.(a) and 4.(b) are set and will not be adjusted.
